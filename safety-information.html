Important Safety Information

Essential safety information for the Versius Surgical System

FDA Marketing Authorization

The Versius Surgical System has received U.S. FDA Marketing Authorization for cholecystectomy procedures.

Indications for Use

The Versius Surgical System is a Robotic-Assisted Surgical Device intended to assist in the precise and accurate control of Versius Surgical endoscopic instruments and is indicated in the U.S. for:

  • Adult patients 22 years of age and older
  • Patients eligible for soft tissue minimal access surgery
  • Cholecystectomy procedures

Important Safety Considerations

Before Using the Versius System

  • Surgeons must complete comprehensive training and certification before operating the system
  • Proper patient selection is critical for successful outcomes
  • All contraindications must be carefully evaluated
  • Operating room setup must follow manufacturer guidelines

During Surgery

  • Continuous visual monitoring of the surgical site is required
  • Backup conventional instruments must be immediately available
  • The surgeon must be prepared to convert to open surgery if necessary
  • All system alarms and warnings must be immediately addressed

Potential Risks and Complications

As with any surgical procedure, the use of the Versius Surgical System may involve risks including but not limited to:

  • Bleeding
  • Infection
  • Organ injury
  • Conversion to open surgery
  • Anesthesia-related complications
  • Equipment malfunction

Training Requirements

All surgeons using the Versius Surgical System must complete:

  • Comprehensive didactic training
  • Hands-on simulation training
  • Proctored clinical cases
  • Ongoing competency assessments

Technical Specifications

System Components

  • Surgeon console with 3D HD visualization
  • Modular robotic arms with 710° range of motion
  • Fully-wristed surgical instruments
  • Patient-side cart with compact footprint

Environmental Requirements

  • Operating room temperature: 18-24°C (64-75°F)
  • Relative humidity: 30-75%
  • Adequate electrical power and backup systems
  • Proper ventilation and air filtration

⚠️ Important Notice

This information is for healthcare professionals only. Patients should consult with their healthcare provider for medical advice.
